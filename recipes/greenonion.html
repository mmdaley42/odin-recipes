Green Onion Pancakes

These Green Onion Pancakes are a delightful nod to the culinary traditions of Taiwan, where they are known as "Cong You Bing." The dish is a perfect blend of simplicity and flavor, featuring layers of flaky dough interspersed with fresh green onions. Each bite offers a satisfying crunch followed by a burst of savory goodness, making it an ideal accompaniment to any meal or a delicious snack on its own. The pancakes are versatile, easily adapted with various fillings or served with dipping sauces, embodying the spirit of comfort food that transcends cultural boundaries.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup boiling water
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 1 cup green onions, finely chopped
  • Additional oil for frying
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the boiling water, stirring with chopsticks or a fork until the mixture forms a shaggy dough.
  2. Add the cold water and knead the dough until smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into four equal pieces. Roll each piece into a thin circle on a lightly floured surface.
  4. Brush each circle with vegetable oil, sprinkle with chopped green onions, and season with salt.
  5. Roll each circle into a log, then coil it into a spiral shape. Flatten gently with your hand.
  6. Heat a skillet over medium heat and add a little oil. Fry each pancake for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove from the skillet and let cool slightly before slicing into wedges. Serve warm with soy sauce or your favorite dipping sauce.
